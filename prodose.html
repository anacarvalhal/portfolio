Overview

><Seeing the dose, in real time

PRO-DOSE is a real-time, in-vivo dosimetry system that lets clinical teams monitor the radiation dose delivered to a patient as a treatment happens - rather than verifying it only afterwards. Multiple dosimeter probes, each mapped to an anatomical reference point, stream live dose-rate readings so the team can confirm the patient is receiving exactly the planned dose.

The product spans the full workflow: defining how a dosimetry session is set up, configuring the dosimeter channels, and then monitoring delivered dose against the planned target throughout treatment. It is an FDA-cleared medical device - a safety-critical, regulated environment where clarity and trust in the interface are essential.

My Role

><Research and design, end to end

I led the full UX effort on Pro-dose - from research through to the design of every screen. Working in a regulated medical context meant grounding each decision in real clinical workflows and validating it against the needs of the people delivering treatment.

Research

><Understanding the clinical workflow

To ground the design in how treatment is actually delivered, the research combined a set of complementary methods - mapping the service, the users, and the safety-critical steps of the dosimetry process. The story map and service blueprint were built in close collaboration with stakeholders, ensuring the workflow reflected real clinical practice.
